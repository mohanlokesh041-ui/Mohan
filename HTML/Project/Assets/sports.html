Sports Issues In Chennai



Major Sports Issues In Chennai

Poor Sports Infrastructure & Maintenance

  • Many public playgrounds and corporation‑run sports fields have equipment that is old, rusted, or broken. Some are locked up for seemingly no reason. The Times of India
  • Synthetic tracks are sometimes delayed or stalled due to legal issues or problems in tender processes. The New Indian Express
  • Playgrounds often lack basic amenities: lighting, fencing, proper flooring, clean changing rooms/restrooms.


Scarcity of Playgrounds Shrinking Open Spaces

  • As urban development intensifies, open spaces and playgrounds are being reduced, repurposed, or encroached upon.
  • In certain neighbourhoods (especially southern Chennai: Pallavaram, Chromepet, Pammal), there are few good quality playgrounds, forcing children to play on streets.


Lack of Facilities for Specific Sports

  • Throwing events (javelin, discus, hammer, etc.) suffer because there are not enough dedicated spaces; many stadiums are used mostly for other sports like football. When it rains, tracks or fields get muddy and unusable.
  • Wheelchair sports and sports for differently‑abled persons: accessibility is poor, awareness is low.
